Mitigating the Hivemind: A Comparative Study of Methods for Increasing Diversity in Open-Ended LLM Generation ongoing

The artificial Hivemind effect refers to the tendency of large language models to produce semantically similar outputs for open-ended queries, limiting their usefulness in creative, pluralistic, and exploratory tasks. This project studies whether culturally grounded examples can serve as a simple inference-time mechanism for diversifying LLM generations. We compare seven interventions, from sampling-level perturbations to a full generation-selection pipeline, across open-ended creative prompts and find a sharp intervention cliff: sampling-level and adapter-level methods do not produce significant diversity gains, while prompt-level in-context learning with culturally diverse examples produces immediate and large improvements. Stacking culturally diverse few-shot examples with cross-response n-gram repulsion and DPP subset selection achieves substantial gains in semantic diversity and effective distinct responses without training, guide models, or knowledge graphs.

Universal Lies: A multi-method analysis of hallucinations

Hallucinations in large language models remain poorly understood at the mechanistic level. Although prior work has shown that LLMs represent the truth and falsehood of statements in hidden-state activations, these findings often rely on simple true/false factual datasets. This project studies the mechanistic origins of this separation under naturalistic hallucination-inducing settings, with particular attention to attention dynamics and individual neurons across model families. We conduct a multi-method empirical survey spanning five models across Qwen2.5, Qwen3, Llama-3.1, and Mistral using TruthfulQA as a common evaluation setting. Our findings suggest that hallucinating models attend significantly less to question-region tokens in late-layer heads, indicating a progressive decoupling from the input query before hallucinated content is produced. Hidden-state geometry shows directional separation consistent with prior work, probes trained on hidden states reach a peak AUC of 0.85 on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, and targeted ablation of hallucination-associated neurons disrupts this geometric structure, implicating them as potential contributors to hallucination-related representations.

Reimplementation: Internal Coherence Maximization for Persona Elicitation
Original paper code Critique
Reimplemented Internal Coherence Maximization (ICM), an unsupervised elicitation method that searches for label assignments that are mutually predictable and internally coherent under a pretrained model. Applied ICM to persona elicitation, where inferred labels can be used as a structured representation of a persona's values, beliefs, or preferences for guiding model behavior. Evaluated whether coherence-based elicitation recovers the target persona's values or instead reinforces pretrained model priors on culturally specific questions.

Genomic Gap Prediction in DNA sequences using Language Models
under Dr. Kapil Ahuja
IIT Indore  ·  Supervised research
In DNA sequences, after being extracted, they may contain unresolved sections, which are often replaced by ambiguous ‘N’s instead of the actual DNA bases (A, T, G, C). In this study, gap imputation is addressed using deep learning methods, including transformers and language modeling.
