Efficient Pluralism: Can Pluralistic Alignment Mitigate the Artificial Hivemind Effect in LLMs?
Replacing LoRA with PrefixMemory Tuning in Modular Pluralism for scalable, diverse LLM responses
Aalekh Bukhariya  ·  George Washington University  ·  2025
Status: ongoing research project
proposal pdf code (coming soon)
Abstract

Post-training methods for LLMs have been observed to exhibit lower diversity in generated responses, as they average out human values across tasks such as poetry writing, humor, and political leanings etc[Sorensen et al., 2024]. Pluralistic alignment methods have been proposed as a solution to this limitation of LLM post training Methods, as they help LLMs incorporate diverse human values. Modular Pluralism is one such method proposed by [Feng et al., 2024]. It trains separate community language models using Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA)[Hu et al., 2022]. Although it is a lightweight method, it is computationally expensive when the number of communities is large. We propose a framework that replaces Low-Rank Adaptation with PrefixMemoryTuning, a lightweight fine-tuning method proposed by [Wang et al., 2026]. We evaluate our method across three pluralistic infer ence modes: Overton, steerable, and distributional, and directly compare against Modular Pluralism on both quality and efficiency metrics. We also benchmark against state-of-the-art models using the INFINITY CHAT dataset from the Artificial Hivemind study [Jiang et al., 2025] to assess whether steerable Pluralism can solve the Artificial Hivemind Problem in LLMs. Results could demonstrate if PrefixMemory-Tuning achieves competitive pluralistic alignment with significantly lower computational requirements, offering a scalable path toward diverse LLM responses.
